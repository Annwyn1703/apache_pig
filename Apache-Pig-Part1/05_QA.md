# Q&A Guide

## Q1
### Question
Why use Apache Pig instead of Apache Spark?

### Answer
Apache Pig is simpler for teaching Hadoop-based batch processing and clearly demonstrates how high-level scripts can replace verbose MapReduce code. Spark is more modern and faster for many workloads, but Pig remains a useful educational tool for understanding the Hadoop ecosystem.

---

## Q2
### Question
What is the difference between Pig and Hive?

### Answer
Hive is SQL-like and best suited to data warehousing queries. Pig is procedural and data-flow oriented, which makes it more flexible for complex transformation pipelines.

---

## Q3
### Question
Why was the RetailRocket dataset selected?

### Answer
It is a real e-commerce interaction dataset with clear user behavior events such as views, add-to-cart actions, and transactions. This makes it suitable for both technical processing and business interpretation.

---

## Q4
### Question
Why is Pig Latin easier than MapReduce?

### Answer
Pig Latin lets developers express transformations directly without writing low-level Java code for mappers, reducers, serialization, and job orchestration.

---

## Q5
### Question
Why use `GROUP ALL` in the first analysis step?

### Answer
`GROUP ALL` places the entire relation into one group so that `COUNT` can return the total number of records in the dataset.

---

## Q6
### Question
Does Pig replace Hadoop?

### Answer
No. Pig runs on top of Hadoop and uses Hadoop execution engines to process the data.

---

## Q7
### Question
What is Pig Latin?

### Answer
Pig Latin is the high-level scripting language used by Apache Pig to define data loading, transformation, aggregation, and storage workflows.

---

## Q8
### Question
What kind of tasks is Pig good at?

### Answer
Pig is well suited to ETL, batch analytics, log processing, and large-scale data transformation tasks.

---

## Q9
### Question
What kind of tasks is Pig not good at?

### Answer
Pig is not ideal for real-time analytics, interactive low-latency applications, or workloads that are better served by newer engines such as Spark.

---

## Q10
### Question
What is lazy evaluation in Pig?

### Answer
Pig delays execution until a trigger command such as `DUMP` or `STORE` is called. This allows it to optimize the execution plan first.

---

## Q11
### Question
Why define a schema when loading the CSV file?

### Answer
The schema tells Pig how to interpret each field, which improves readability and supports correct transformation logic later in the script.

---

## Q12
### Question
What happens if the schema is wrong?

### Answer
The analysis may produce incorrect results, type errors, or misleading outputs. That is why `DESCRIBE` and sample validation are important early steps.

---

## Q13
### Question
Why is `events.csv` the main file in the demo?

### Answer
It contains the core event log needed to analyze customer behavior. It provides the most direct path from raw data to business questions.

---

## Q14
### Question
What does the `view` event mean?

### Answer
It indicates that a visitor viewed a product, which is a signal of customer interest.

---

## Q15
### Question
What does the `addtocart` event mean?

### Answer
It indicates purchase intent because the customer selected an item for possible checkout.

---

## Q16
### Question
What does the `transaction` event mean?

### Answer
It represents a completed purchase and is the strongest direct business signal in the dataset.

---

## Q17
### Question
Why is event distribution useful?

### Answer
It reveals how customers move through the funnel from interest to purchase and helps identify where drop-off occurs.

---

## Q18
### Question
Why analyze top viewed products?

### Answer
This helps identify customer interest patterns and supports recommendations, promotions, and inventory planning.

---

## Q19
### Question
Why analyze top active visitors?

### Answer
This helps measure engagement and can support segmentation, retention strategies, and CRM-related analysis.

---

## Q20
### Question
Does high activity always mean high revenue?

### Answer
No. A visitor may generate many events without completing a transaction. Activity and purchase value should be analyzed separately.

---

## Q21
### Question
Why use `FILTER` before finding top products?

### Answer
Because the question is specifically about viewed products. Filtering keeps the analysis aligned with the business objective.

---

## Q22
### Question
Why use `ORDER` and `LIMIT` together?

### Answer
`ORDER` ranks the results, and `LIMIT` selects the highest-ranked subset such as the top 10 products or visitors.

---

## Q23
### Question
Why store the results in HDFS?

### Answer
Stored outputs can be reused by dashboards, reports, later ETL tasks, or other analytical workflows.

---

## Q24
### Question
Can Pig work with data other than CSV?

### Answer
Yes. Pig can work with structured and semi-structured data, and it supports more complex types such as tuples, bags, and maps.

---

## Q25
### Question
What are tuples, bags, and maps in Pig?

### Answer
They are Pig’s built-in complex data structures. Tuples represent records, bags represent collections of tuples, and maps store key-value pairs.

---

## Q26
### Question
Why is Pig still worth studying if Spark is more popular?

### Answer
Pig helps students understand the evolution of Big Data tools and shows how high-level abstractions simplified Hadoop-based analytics before newer engines became dominant.

---

## Q27
### Question
What is the advantage of Pig for teaching?

### Answer
It makes distributed batch processing concepts easier to explain than raw MapReduce while still staying close to the Hadoop ecosystem.

---

## Q34
### Question
How can the demo fail?

### Answer
Common issues include incorrect HDFS paths, schema mismatches, missing input files, or output directories that already exist before a `STORE` command.

---

## Q35
### Question
What is the backup plan if the cluster fails during the presentation?

### Answer
Use prepared screenshots, static output tables, charts, and a short prerecorded run of the demo while continuing the explanation.

---

## Q36
### Question
What is the difference between batch analytics and real-time analytics?

### Answer
Batch analytics processes accumulated data in groups, while real-time analytics processes data with much lower latency as events arrive.

---

## Q37
### Question
Why is Apache Pig considered an ETL tool?

### Answer
Because it is strong at extracting data, transforming it through multi-step workflows, and loading the processed output into storage systems.

---

## Q38

Yes. Pig supports user-defined functions, which allows developers to extend built-in capabilities when custom logic is required.
